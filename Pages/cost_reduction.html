Hit your cost down targets on time - for the first time.

CoLab gives you a platform to bring your whole team into a VA/VE event, so anyone can access drawings, files and 3D models. Meaning you get more ideas from more team members faster than ever before.

Schedule a demo

How it works

Bring idea generation from in-person to online

CoLab is a browser-based platform where anyone on your team can access a VA/VE review package with 3D CAD models and 2D files. Before, you had to get everyone in one room to run a cost down event. With CoLab, you have an easy way to host virtual or hybrid events. So, you can spend less money on travel and more time generating quality ideas

1 app > 5 disconnected workflows

Most teams do design-to-cost in some combination of: meetings, PowerPoint decks, spreadsheets, email, project management software and/or sticky notes. And because none of these “systems” talk to each other, you end up with a lot of disconnected ideas and very little context. Teams who use CoLab pin design-to-cost feedback directly on 3D models, so any team member can understand the “Why” behind the idea and respond in real-time. Now, you can connect ideas to the model, respond to feedback and track the entire design-to-cost project in a single platform.

Execute the cost reduction ideas that matter

It’s not that your team doesn’t have great ideas for reducing costs or improving a product’s value. It’s that prioritizing and executing those ideas is a bottleneck.

Engineering teams who use CoLab generate ideas in the context of the model, then prioritize the highest value ideas across the entire VA/VE event. Within the event, a participant can:

  • Leave VA/VE ideas on the 3D model
  • Tag ideas by function and prioritize that idea by impact
  • Sort all feedback by potential impact and functional tags

This means less follow-up, less post-event prioritization and faster execution on the cost reduction ideas that matter.

Integrations

You don’t need yet another disconnected software solution. That's why CoLab does two things:

  • It integrates with your CAD and PLM software to create a connected tech ecosystem.
  • It replaces the 5-10 apps you use to complete a single process workflow with 1-2 apps (CoLab + PLM, for example)

    • CoLab supports more than 70 file types, including native and neutral CAD and integrates with many PLM/PDM systems. Aside from engineering system integrations, CoLab also integrates with Jira and supports security protocols, like SSO, user permissions and more.

FAQs about using CoLab for VA/VE

I believe we can generate more ideas using CoLab, but does that actually mean more savings?
+
We can’t speak to your unique situation, but the customers who use CoLab to generate more ideas during cost down events also report significant savings.

Now, sometimes the company didn’t track the number of ideas to cost savings prior to implementing CoLab. But, now they can. That’s also considered a win for their VA/VE events.
We do cost savings all the time but our group has a hard time tracking how ideas lead to results and then quantifying that result. Can CoLab help us do that?
+
In short: maybe. It all depends on your level of commitment to the metrics that matter during your cost reduction event.

Can CoLab quantify ideas-to-results, yes! Does it need inputs from you to do it, like part costs? Also, yes! However, the actual process to start doing this in CoLab is surprisingly simple.

First, by acting as a single source for cost down feedback, CoLab houses and analyzes all ideas stemming from a VA/VE event. This can be done in the context of the model or drawing or in the form of a downloadable idea audit log. From here, customers who use this feedback trail track how each idea moves through the idea pipeline to actual implementation. Finally, by assessing the part costs, increased product value or added function, teams translate that feedback to measurable results.
Is CoLab for teams that are new to VA/VE? Or do you also work with mature VA/VE programs?
+
Yes. CoLab works with customers who have done everything from start a fledgling cost reduction initiative to optimize a fully mature VA/VE program. The difference is a matter of speed and scale.

For teams new to VA/VE, the initial rollout is small and focused on time-to-value. Successful leading indicators might be the number of cost reduction ideas generated, the number of team members submitting ideas or the successful completion of a VA/VE event. Throughout your partnership with CoLab, the plan to get to your goals evolves as your team’s use of CoLab evolves.

For teams with mature VA/VE programs, the major goals are bringing VA/VE events and generated ideas into a single platform.

1. Standardize the cost reduction process in a single platform. This helps teams generate more ideas and prioritize those ideas all in CoLab.
2. Bring more people to the idea generation process. As a browser-based platform, CoLab can be used by anyone with a computer and an internet connection.
We don't do cost down today because, frankly, it takes time and we're swamped -- is this going to be easy enough that we actually do it?
+
It would be too easy for us to say, “Yes!” here and move on. But this question is more nuanced and it depends on your commitment to change.

Do teams comment on the ease of use of CoLab for VA/VE? All the time. But they’re also committed to the outcomes.

Outcomes are a critical component of CoLab’s onboarding process. Prior to training your team, you and your Customer Success Manager will sit down and map out what you hope to achieve with CoLab and how you’ll actually get there.

All this to say, CoLab was made for engineers by engineers. Time-to-value and ease of use are what we want customers to experience every time they use CoLab.
How does CoLab fit into today's process? Will it totally replace today's spreadsheet or PowerPoint?
+
Yes! CoLab is meant to replace the spreadsheets, PowerPoints and emails your team loves to hate. Of course that doesn’t mean you have to burn the spreadsheet the moment you implement CoLab.

The onboarding process includes a step called process mapping. During this step, your CoLab Customer Success Manager will review - with you - the exact process for how you do VA/VE today. They will then map this process into CoLab and train your team on the new process.

This is a critical point. CoLab is not a software vendor – we’re a software partner. We’ve helped many companies successfully move from spreadsheets to CoLab and will help your team along the way.
How does this fit into our ECR/ECO process?
+
Depending on the sophistication of your ECR/ECO process, CoLab can fit in a big or small way.

If your process is largely digital today and housed in a PLM, then CoLab helps in two major ways:
1. Capturing the ECN/ECO conversations happening outside PLM in a single platform.
2. Uploading those conversations into PLM as a reference doc on the ECO.

Overall, CoLab will not interfere with your team’s ability to execute engineering changes in PLM. Instead, CoLab will enable your multidisciplinary teams to evaluate and review more ECRs, more effectively, in less time.
We do a business case before implementing an idea. Does CoLab support that?
+
In most instances, we’ll recommend you keep the business case portion of your VA/VE process in the system you use today.

While we can calculate potential savings using unit cost inputs and effort versus impact, this is better for prioritizing the ideas generated during a cost reduction event.

Once you have a list of ideas and their accompanying business cases, you can go back into CoLab and download an audit log of the ideas.
We have plenty of ideas, our challenge is getting them to cost realization on time. How does CoLab reduce that time?
+
This all depends on where the bottlenecks lie in your current process. Customers who struggled with this pre-CoLab experienced challenges like scattered feedback, file overload, and lack of external collaboration.

These are the exact problems CoLab helps solve.

For some teams, it's a condensed version of their stage-gate process. If that’s you, we have ideas on how to move the design review work into CoLab to speed it up and drive more accountability.
Sometimes you need to be in person to hold a product. Is the quality of a virtual VA/VE event going to be as good as in person?
+
CoLab gives you the option to host completely virtual events, but you can also use CoLab to improve the outcomes of your in-person events. Many of our customers do both.

In the context of an in-person event, you may want to use CoLab to document the ideas your team generates.

1. By pinning ideas to a 3D model, you capture much more context.
2. Because everyone has their own account, it's easy to see who came up with which idea.

Some customers also use virtual events in CoLab as a "pre-read" for their in-person events, which results in a more thorough teardown on site.
