Document and track your design review process before an audit.

Without an automated system for tracking the design review process, engineering teams risk a corrective action during every audit. With CoLab, teams automatically track every step in a design review process and can mark every step Pass/Fail depending on the requirements. This checklist can then be uploaded to PLM as a PDF to appease any auditor.

GET A DEMO

How it works

Standardize your design review process – so when audits arise, your team is ready

Design review is often neglected as part of a formal engineering documentation process. Engineering teams have it as a line item, but when it’s time for an audit or a failure analysis, there’s no individual steps to point to as the failure point. Instead, teams who use CoLab to run design reviews create a standardized process just by using the tool.

Here’s how it works:

  • Design review lead kicks off the review inside CoLab – complete with 2D drawings and 3D models
  • Team members access those files in CoLab via secure email link
  • Team members can manipulate 3D models and pin comments to problem areas
  • Once a review is complete, the design review lead uses those comments to revise the original files

Now, when it’s time for an audit, not only does the engineering team have a standard design review process, they have a single system that captures this automatically.

Speed up the ECO process and appease your ISO requirements

That ECR backlog still piling up? It’s not surprising when engineering teams don’t transfer ECRs to ECOs. Once an ECO is created, the ISO quality process starts. And those processes have strict requirements and stricter deadlines.

The last thing your team needs is a corrective action.

CoLab helps teams tackle that ECR backlog by capturing the ECO feedback that gets lost in emails, meetings, and “Hey you gotta second?”

Using CoLab, engineering teams reduce change cycle time by giving cross-functional teams a faster way to review change requests and action change orders.

So, you can (finally) check off those unactioned change requests lingering in your PLM.

Get critical product data out of email and into a trackable platform

Despite its ease, email is not a secure file-sharing platform. Meaning every file sent via email is a surefire way to fail a documents handling audit.

With CoLab, teams bring all design file sharing into a single platform – so every design decision is traceable.

By pinning comments directly onto models and drawings, responding to design feedback within comment threads and tracking design decisions through to completion – all within CoLab – your critical product decisions no longer live in random inboxes.

Instead, you have a secure, trackable history of design feedback you can search in seconds.

Integrations

You don't need yet another disconnected software solution. That's why CoLab does two things:

  • integrates with your CAD and PLM software to create a connected tech ecosystem.
  • it replaces the 5-10 apps you use to complete a single process workflow with 1-2 apps (CoLab + PLM, for example)

FAQs about using CoLab for documentation

We have standard checklists for ISO, PPAP and other industry standards. Can we upload these into CoLab?
+
Yes – you can upload any preexisting checklists into CoLab as a CSV. This will then be converted to a checklist inside the CoLab interface.

Pinning feedback while a checklist item is open will then link that piece of feedback with the checklist item, making it easier to understand which comments support each item on your checklist.

Each checklist step also has a Pass or Fail qualification. So, if the pinned design feedback indicates a passed checklist step, a designated team members can check it off. And the same if a step fails

.Checklist statuses can be viewed in list or Kanban as a general status check for the list. Checklists can also be exported for record-keeping as a CSV with all relevant design data and references enclosed.

I get that CoLab can help us house design review feedback, but how does that work and how can I action it?
+
One of the major goals for teams using CoLab is to access a historical record of design decisions. Often teams in this case have assemblies that are 5-10 years old with no explanation of why they’re assembled the way they’re assembled.

Each checklist item can have feedback attached directly in the 3D view or drawing. That way, evidence for why something passed or failed is always captured exactly where it matters — on the part or document.

With CoLab, design feedback is saved as pins on a model or file. Within this pin may be:

- Responses from team members
- Additional files as attachments
- Feedback type to indicate an issue, idea, task, comment or markup
- Tags to designate feedback type or priority
- Status to indicate where this feedback stands in the greater review

This feedback is automatically saved in your Workspace once it’s posted.

How does this fit into our current ECR/ECO process?
+
Depending on the sophistication of your ECR/ECO process, CoLab can fit in a big or small way.If your process is largely digital today and housed in a PLM, then CoLab helps in two major ways:

1. Capturing the ECN/ECO conversations happening outside PLM in a single platform. These conversations today are likely happening in meetings, email or over Teams. And because none of it’s trackable, none of it’s actionable. Leading to long product development timelines and an ever-increasing queue of ECRs. Companies who use CoLab to progress ECRs see faster time to ECO, reduced ECR queue buildup and more external stakeholders involved in the ECO process.

Data like ECR rejection reasons, review comments and alternative solutions that were not selected are lost during the ECO process. PLM only captures the data necessary for the ECR to move forward.

Overall, CoLab will not interfere with your team’s ability to execute engineering changes in PLM.

Instead, CoLab enables your multidisciplinary teams to evaluate and review more ECRs, more effectively, in less time – and will add traceability to the supporting conversations that PLM can’t capture.

If your ECO process is still primarily paper-based, then CoLab can work similarly to replace much of the random, untrackable systems your team uses today. During onboarding, your Customer Success Manager can discuss options to move your current ECO process out of paper and into CoLab.
As the product evolves, we run into the issue where our customer modifies requirements late in the process. At delivery, the development is aligned to what the customer requested. But then the original documentation is off and our quality team can’t properly validate the final product. Can CoLab help alleviate these pains?
+
To an extent, yes. Where CoLab can help here is how the customer requests changes and how those change requests are tracked. Companies who use CoLab can invite customers or other external parties into a design review free of charge. This means any customer feedback, like a design change, is automatically tracked in the system. This can then be actioned by your team, including changing any requirements and validation documentation.While the original requirements and validation documents cannot be edited directly in CoLab, they can be uploaded as a file in the review. And any changes can be re-uploaded as a revision of the original file. This means everyone on your team and your customer stays up-to-date on any project changes.
I don’t want to add any more complexity to our engineering processes. How does CoLab help us eliminate documentation?
+
It depends on how you document processes today. Often, we find engineers use a combination of spreadsheets, emails and notes in a PowerPoint to document design feedback.

Not only is it time-consuming to update these channels, it’s nearly impossible to track down a single piece of design feedback.

CoLab replaces these manual, untrackable channels. By taking your team out of spreadsheets, emails and PowerPoint meetings, not only are you saving your team time – you’re also eliminating the need to document feedback in these places.

CoLab automatically tracks these design conversations and decisions in a single platform. This is all discoverable via a filterable list, a Kanban board or a running design feedback feed.

This is then exportable so you can save it to your PLM or records database.
